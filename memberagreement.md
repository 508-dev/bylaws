## **508.dev Member Agreement**

Welcome to 508.dev.

508 is a transparency-driven, democratic software engineering co-op, organized as a multi-member limited liability company taxed as a partnership. Membership is a commitment — not just to do good work, but to operate according to our shared values.

By becoming a member, you agree to the following:


---

### **1. Commitment to 508 Values**

Members agree to operate in alignment with the canonical [508.dev values](https://wiki.508.dev/s/values), including:

* Transparency
* Free (as in Freedom) Software
* Progression and Personal Responsibility
* Flexibility
* "Never Wrong to Do the Right Thing"

These values guide our decisions, projects, compensation, and conduct.


---

### **2. Professional Conduct & Respect**

508.dev maintains a professional, respectful, and safe environment.

Members agree to:

* Treat all members, clients, and collaborators with respect.
* Avoid harassment, discrimination, intimidation, or retaliation.
* Raise concerns constructively and in good faith.
* Accept feedback without hostility.

Harassment or abusive behavior toward members, clients, or community participants will not be tolerated.


---

### **3. No Malicious or Harmful Actions**

Members agree not to engage in malicious or harmful conduct toward:

* 508.dev infrastructure, finances, or reputation
* Other members
* Clients
* Shared codebases or systems

This includes sabotage, theft, intentional misrepresentation, misuse of access, or actions intended to harm the co-op or its members. Disagreements are normal. Malicious behavior is not.


---

### **4. Transparency & Accountability**

Transparency is core to 508.

Members agree to:

* Share relevant project information openly within 508.
* Be transparent about capabilities, concerns, and blockers.
* Disclose conflicts of interest.
* Participate in financial transparency practices.

No member will be punished for raising engineering, ethical, or operational concerns in good faith.


---

### **5. Governance & Decision Making**

508.dev operates as a cooperative guided by transparency, open discussion, and good-faith consensus.

Members are encouraged to actively participate in discussions that shape the direction of projects, policies, and cooperative initiatives. Those who engage in governance discussions help shape decisions.

Consensus generally means broad agreement among engaged members, without sustained unresolved objections. Where consensus cannot be reached, decisions proceed to a formal vote as described in the bylaws.

Formal governance structure, legal authority, and administrative procedures are defined in the [508.dev bylaws](/doc/draft-bylaws-RVyqdheQIK).

Members designated as Manager(s) of Record are responsible for financial administration, legal compliance, and operational continuity, and may take necessary administrative action to protect the cooperative's legal, financial, or operational stability. This role is administrative only and does not override a duly adopted member vote.

Outside of those legal and administrative responsibilities, members participate as equals in shaping the cooperative.


---

### **6. Economic Participation & Tax Treatment**

508 is a multi-member LLC taxed as a partnership. Economic participation is based on labor contribution, not capital ownership — there is no cash buy-in required to join.

Members agree to:

* Understand that surplus is allocated as patronage distributions proportional to labor contribution, not capital investment, as detailed in the bylaws (Article V).
* Receive an IRS Schedule K-1 annually reflecting their distributive share of the cooperative's profit or loss, and handle their own income and self-employment tax obligations arising from it, including any estimated tax payments.
* Provide any tax forms reasonably requested by the cooperative (e.g., Form W-9 or the applicable Form W-8), particularly members residing outside the United States, who may be subject to U.S. withholding on effectively connected income.
* Understand that the cooperative's shared capital (the Collective Account) — including value contributed by the founding member upon conversion from a single-member LLC — is held collectively and is not owned by, or redeemable to, any individual member.

Members are strongly encouraged to consult their own tax professional regarding their individual obligations. This agreement does not constitute tax advice.


---

### **7. Independence & Professional Standards**

508 is fully remote and self-organizing.

Members are expected to:

* Take initiative in managing their work.
* Maintain organized tickets and project boards.
* Uphold high engineering standards.
* Seek improvement and mentorship actively.

Members are expected to take initiative and contribute responsibly to cooperative projects.


---

### **8. Client Relationships & Fair Referral Practices**

508 operates as a cooperative, and shared opportunity is part of that model.

If a client engagement originates through 508.dev, members agree to:

* Inform 508 of any direct hire or independent contract discussions.
* Act in good faith to ensure the co-op is treated fairly.
* Work with 508 leadership to determine an equitable referral or transition arrangement where appropriate.

This is not a non-compete. Members are free professionals. However, intentionally circumventing 508 referral structures in bad faith undermines the cooperative and may result in review of membership.


---

### **9. Confidentiality**

While we are radically transparent internally, client confidentiality must be respected.

Members agree to:

* Protect confidential client information.
* Follow agreed-upon NDAs or contractual obligations.
* Avoid sharing client-sensitive information publicly without permission.

Confidentiality obligations continue after a member leaves 508.


---

### **10. Intellectual Property**

508 works on client projects and cooperative initiatives. Clear ownership protects everyone.

**Client Work**

Work created under 508.dev client engagements follows the applicable client agreement. Members agree to assign or license rights as necessary to fulfill those agreements.

**Cooperative Assets**

Software, tooling, documentation, branding, and systems developed specifically for 508 belong to 508.dev unless otherwise agreed. Where appropriate, these will follow 508's Free Software principles (GPLv3/AGPLv3 by default).

**Member Work**

Members retain ownership of intellectual property created outside of 508 engagements. Members are responsible for ensuring they have the right to contribute any materials used in 508 projects.

Members agree not to misuse, misappropriate, or improperly claim ownership of client or cooperative intellectual property.


---

### **11. Resolution & Membership Review**

If a member violates this agreement or the core values of 508:

* The issue will be discussed openly and in good faith.
* Depending on severity, membership may be suspended or revoked following the process in the bylaws (Article VII).
* The cooperative retains the right to remove members who act in ways materially harmful to 508 or its values.


---

508 is built on trust, transparency, and shared growth.

Membership means committing not just to engineering excellence, but to fairness, honesty, and collective progress.

### Definitions

**Member**: An individual who has been admitted into 508.dev under the cooperative's membership process (bylaws Article III) and has agreed to this Member Agreement.

**Co-op / 508.dev**: The 508.dev cooperative organization and its members collectively, organized as a Wyoming multi-member LLC taxed as a partnership.

**Leadership / Governance**: The group or process defined by the cooperative for operational or governance decisions, including any Member(s) designated as Manager(s) of Record.

**Capital Account**: A member's individual tax-accounting record of contributions, allocated profit/loss, and distributions, maintained per the bylaws (Article V). It is not transferable equity.

**Collective Account**: The cooperative's shared, non-distributable capital, including value contributed by the founding member upon conversion from a single-member LLC. It is not owned by, or redeemable to, any individual member.

**Patronage Distribution**: A surplus distribution to a member proportional to their labor contribution, rather than any capital ownership.
