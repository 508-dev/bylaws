BYLAWS OF 508.DEV COOPERATIVE

*A Wyoming multi-member limited liability company operating as a worker cooperative*

---

## PREFATORY NOTE

These Bylaws govern 508.dev Cooperative ("508," "the Cooperative," or "the Company"), a Wyoming limited liability company taxed as a partnership for U.S. federal income tax purposes. These Bylaws operate together with the Cooperative's Operating Agreement — the document recognized under the Wyoming Limited Liability Company Act, Wyo. Stat. § 17-29-101 et seq. for purposes of state filings, banking, and third-party dealings — and with the [Member Agreement](/doc/draft-member-agreement-BIZFA9Xfi4).

Where these Bylaws and the Operating Agreement conflict on a narrow matter of statutory or third-party formality (e.g., who is recognized as a signatory by a bank or counterparty), the Operating Agreement controls for that limited purpose. In all other respects — internal governance, membership, economic participation, and cooperative practice — these Bylaws are the controlling and authoritative document among Members.

These Bylaws are not a substitute for legal, tax, or accounting advice. Members are encouraged to consult independent counsel and a qualified accountant, particularly regarding partnership tax treatment (IRS Form 1065 and Schedule K-1 reporting), self-employment tax, and withholding obligations applicable to non-U.S. Members under the Internal Revenue Code (including withholding on effectively connected income allocable to foreign partners under IRC § 1446).

---

## **ARTICLE I — NAME, PURPOSE & LEGAL STRUCTURE**

### **Section 1.1 — Name**

The name of the cooperative shall be **508.dev Cooperative** ("the Cooperative").

### **Section 1.2 — Legal Structure**

The Cooperative is organized as a multi-member limited liability company under the Wyoming Limited Liability Company Act, Wyo. Stat. § 17-29-101 et seq., and is taxed as a partnership for U.S. federal income tax purposes under the entity classification ("check-the-box") rules of Treasury Regulation § 301.7701-3, unless the Members elect otherwise by a vote meeting the threshold in Section 4.1(b).

The Cooperative was originally organized as a single-member LLC and converted to a multi-member LLC effective **[CONVERSION DATE]**, pursuant to Article XI. These Bylaws govern member rights, voting authority, and economic participation irrespective of statutory form.

### **Section 1.3 — Purpose**

The Cooperative exists to:

1. Provide software engineering and related services.
2. Operate as a transparency-driven, democratic worker cooperative.
3. Advance Free (as in Freedom) Software.
4. Provide fair compensation and shared economic participation to revenue-generating members.
5. Operate in alignment with the Cooperative's stated values.

The Cooperative shall operate in accordance with internationally recognized cooperative principles, including democratic member control and member economic participation, to the extent consistent with its status as a Wyoming LLC taxed as a partnership.

### **Section 1.4 — Principal Office, Registered Agent & Membership Location**

The Cooperative's registered office and registered agent in Wyoming shall be as designated in the Operating Agreement and on file with the Wyoming Secretary of State. The Cooperative's actual place of business is fully remote, and Members may reside in any jurisdiction, consistent with the international membership commitments in Article XI, Section 11.3.

---

## **ARTICLE II — COOPERATIVE PRINCIPLES**

508.dev affirms the following governing principles:

1. Democratic control (one member, one vote).
2. Transparency of finances and operations.
3. Equitable distribution of surplus.
4. Voluntary and open membership.
5. Education and advancement of members.
6. Commitment to ethical conduct and "Never Wrong to Do the Right Thing."

---

## **ARTICLE III — MEMBERSHIP**

### **Section 3.1 — Definitions**

(a) **"Member"** means an individual admitted to membership under Section 3.3, who has executed the Member Agreement and remains in good standing under these Bylaws.

(b) **"Contributor"** means an individual who works with or generates revenue through the Cooperative but has not yet been admitted as a Member. Contributors are not Members and hold no voting or economic rights under these Bylaws, but may be compensated for their work under separate arrangement.

(c) **"Departing Member"** means a Member whose membership has ended by resignation, removal, incapacity, or death.

(d) **"Membership Interest"** or **"Unit"** means the single, non-economic, non-transferable governance interest held by each Member, described further in Article VIII. A Unit is distinct from a Member's Capital Account (Article V, Section 5.2), which is a tax-accounting construct and does not itself represent transferable equity or a claim on the Collective Account.

### **Section 3.2 — Eligibility**

Membership is open to individuals who:

* Generate revenue through the Cooperative, or contribute materially to Cooperative operations; and
* Agree to the Member Agreement and the [508.dev values](https://wiki.508.dev/s/values); and
* Complete the admission process in Section 3.3.

Membership shall not be denied on arbitrary or discriminatory grounds.

### **Section 3.3 — Admission Process**

1. **Expression of interest.** The Contributor or an existing Member proposes the Contributor for membership.
2. **Contribution period.** The Contributor works with the Cooperative for a period of active, material contribution (a default of 60 days of active contribution is suggested; the Members may waive or adjust this by ordinary vote under Section 4.1(a) without amending these Bylaws).
3. **Orientation.** The Contributor confirms they have read and agree to the 508.dev values, the Member Agreement, and these Bylaws.
4. **Review.** Designated recruiting Members review the Contributor's fit and contribution.
5. **Admission vote.** Admission is approved by the recruiting Members and does not take effect over the documented, timely objection of Members representing more than one-quarter of the existing Membership.
6. **Execution.** The new Member executes the Member Agreement and is recorded in the Cooperative's membership register, effective on the date of execution.

Members may, by ordinary vote, waive any part of this process for a given Contributor.

### **Section 3.4 — Rights of Members**

Each Member shall have:

* One equal vote in Cooperative governance.
* Access to full financial transparency, including the Cooperative's aggregate financial records and their own Capital Account statement.
* Participation in surplus (patronage) allocation decisions.
* The right to raise concerns without retaliation.
* The right to propose motions and initiatives.
* The right to call for a vote under Section 4.1 or a special meeting under Section 4.2.

Membership rights may not be overridden by subcontracting agreements or by any designation of statutory Manager of Record under Article VI.

### **Section 3.5 — Responsibilities of Members**

Members must:

* Uphold Cooperative values.
* Act in good faith.
* Maintain professional standards.
* Protect client confidentiality.
* Participate in governance.
* Provide any tax forms or documentation reasonably required of them under Section 5.6.

### **Section 3.6 — Non-Transferability**

No Member may sell, assign, pledge, or otherwise transfer their Membership Interest or any right arising from it. Any attempted transfer is void and confers no rights on the intended transferee. New members are admitted only through the process in Section 3.3, never by acquiring an existing Member's interest.

### **Section 3.7 — Maintaining Membership; Inactivity**

A Member who ceases to generate revenue through, or materially contribute to, the Cooperative for six consecutive months without an approved leave of absence under Section 3.8 may have their membership status reviewed under Article VII. Review under this section is not automatic expulsion — it triggers the process in Section 7.2, and the Member retains full rights until a vote concludes that process.

### **Section 3.8 — Leaves of Absence**

A Member may request a leave of absence (e.g., for medical, family, or personal reasons) without losing membership. Leaves are approved by ordinary vote of the Members and should specify an expected duration and any adjustment to expectations of contribution or distributions during the leave.

## **ARTICLE IV — GOVERNANCE**

### **Section 4.1 — Voting**

Each Member shall have one vote. Decisions shall be made by:

(a) Simple majority for ordinary business.

(b) Two-thirds majority for:

* Removal of a Member
* Amendments to these Bylaws
* Dissolution
* Changes to the surplus distribution structure
* Changes to the Cooperative's tax classification or legal structure

Voting may occur asynchronously via documented electronic systems. Members are expected to cast votes within 7 days of a matter being called for a vote; a Member's failure to vote within that window is not counted toward either the majority or the total for purposes of reaching the required threshold, but does not block a decision from taking effect once the threshold is met among those who voted. Quorum for any vote is a majority of all Members in good standing.

### **Section 4.2 — Meetings**

* An annual all-Member meeting shall be held at least once per calendar year.
* Regular governance meetings shall occur at least quarterly.
* Financial transparency reports shall be presented at least quarterly.
* Special meetings may be called by any 25% of Members, on no less than 5 days' notice to all Members, except in matters of genuine urgency.
* Meetings may be held in person, by teleconference, or asynchronously via a documented electronic system, at the discretion of whoever calls the meeting.

### **Section 4.3 — Decision-Making Process**

Members shall seek good-faith consensus before resorting to a formal vote. Any Member may raise a proposal for discussion. If the group has not reached consensus within 14 days of a proposal being raised, or if any Member requests a formal vote at any time, the matter proceeds to a vote under Section 4.1.

### **Section 4.4 — Proxies**

A Member unable to participate in a given vote may designate another Member in writing to cast a proxy vote on their behalf for that specific matter. A proxy vote counts as the delegating Member's own vote for purposes of quorum and outcome.

### **Section 4.5 — Records of Governance**

Minutes, decisions, and vote outcomes shall be documented and made accessible to all Members within 5 days of a meeting or vote concluding.

## **ARTICLE V — ECONOMIC PARTICIPATION**

### **Section 5.1 — Revenue**

All Cooperative revenue shall be deposited into Cooperative accounts and recorded transparently.

### **Section 5.2 — Tax Classification & Capital Accounts**

The Cooperative is taxed as a partnership under Subchapter K of the Internal Revenue Code. Each Member has a Capital Account maintained in accordance with Treasury Regulation § 1.704-1(b)(2)(iv), reflecting the Member's contributions (if any), allocated share of profit and loss, and distributions. Because membership is based on labor contribution rather than capital investment, a new Member's opening Capital Account balance is zero, except as provided in Section 5.3.

### **Section 5.3 — Collective Account**

The Cooperative maintains a permanent **Collective Account** representing shared, non-distributable cooperative capital. Upon conversion from a single-member to a multi-member LLC, the assets, working capital, and retained value of the Cooperative accumulated prior to conversion were contributed to the Collective Account rather than credited to any individual Member's Capital Account — including that of the founding Member. The Collective Account is not owned by, and is not redeemable to, any individual Member. Contributions to and disbursements from the Collective Account (e.g., for reserves or shared infrastructure) require the vote specified in Section 5.7.

### **Section 5.4 — Surplus (Profits)**

Surplus is defined as revenue minus:

* Member compensation and draws
* Operational expenses
* Reserves
* Taxes

### **Section 5.5 — Surplus (Patronage) Allocation**

Surplus shall be allocated according to Member vote and may include:

* Patronage distributions proportional to each Member's labor contribution (e.g., hours worked or revenue generated) during the relevant period.
* Retained earnings credited to the Collective Account for Cooperative growth.
* Investment in shared tools or infrastructure.

No surplus may be distributed on the basis of capital ownership. Absent a contrary vote, patronage distributions shall be calculated and declared at least annually, following the close of the fiscal year.

### **Section 5.6 — Distribution Timing & Tax Reporting**

Each Member shall receive an IRS Schedule K-1 reflecting their distributive share of the Cooperative's profit or loss for the applicable tax year. Members are individually responsible for their own income and self-employment tax obligations arising from that distributive share, including any estimated tax payments. Members residing outside the United States acknowledge that the Cooperative may be required to withhold and remit U.S. tax on effectively connected income allocable to them (including under IRC § 1446) and agree to timely provide any tax forms (e.g., Form W-9 or the applicable Form W-8) reasonably requested by the Cooperative to determine or reduce such withholding.

### **Section 5.7 — Reserves**

The Members shall, by ordinary vote, set and periodically review a target operating reserve to be maintained in the Collective Account.

### **Section 5.8 — Transparency**

All financial records, including:

* Salaries and draws
* Bill rates
* Expenses
* Profit/patronage distributions
* Each Member's own Capital Account statement

shall be accessible to all Members.

## **ARTICLE VI — MANAGEMENT STRUCTURE**

### **Section 6.1 — Operational Roles**

The Cooperative may designate operational roles (e.g., Managing Member, Treasurer, Project Leads, Recruitment Leads, Business Development Leads), but such roles do not override democratic control.

### **Section 6.2 — Manager(s) of Record**

For purposes of Wyoming law, banking, contracting, and other third-party dealings requiring a recognized signatory, the Cooperative shall designate one or more Members as "Manager(s)" or "Authorized Signatories" of record in the Operating Agreement. This designation is administrative only and confers no governance authority beyond what is explicitly delegated by Member vote. Manager(s) of record shall be reaffirmed at least annually by ordinary vote and may be removed at any time by the same.

### **Section 6.3 — Authority**

No individual may unilaterally:

* Change compensation structures.
* Distribute surplus.
* Amend bylaws.
* Admit or remove members.
* Bind the Cooperative to obligations outside the ordinary course of business or a documented budget, without Member approval.

### **Section 6.4 — Fiduciary Duty of Manager(s) of Record**

A Member serving as Manager of Record under Section 6.2 owes the Cooperative and its Members good faith, loyalty, and care in exercising the administrative authority described there, and shall act consistently with duly adopted Member votes except where legally required to do otherwise (see Article XI, Section 11.2).

---

## **ARTICLE VII — MEMBER REVIEW & REMOVAL**

### **Section 7.1 — Grounds**

A Member may be subject to review for:

* Malicious conduct.
* Financial misconduct.
* Harassment or discrimination.
* Material violation of Cooperative values or the Member Agreement.
* Sustained inactivity without an approved leave of absence (Section 3.7).

### **Section 7.2 — Process**

(a) **Notice of concern.** Written notice describing the specific concern is delivered to the Member.

(b) **Opportunity for response.** The Member has no less than 14 days to respond in writing and/or address the Membership directly.

(c) **Good-faith discussion.** Members attempt to resolve the concern in good faith before proceeding to a vote.

(d) **Vote.** Removal requires a two-thirds majority of all other Members. The Member subject to review does not vote on their own removal but retains all other rights, including the right to be heard, until the vote concludes.

### **Section 7.3 — Effect of Removal or Withdrawal**

Upon removal, resignation, incapacity, or death, a Member's voting and governance rights cease immediately. Any amounts owed under Article VIII are settled per Section 8.4. Confidentiality and intellectual property obligations under the Member Agreement survive termination of membership.

---

## **ARTICLE VIII — CAPITAL & OWNERSHIP**

### **Section 8.1 — Basis of Membership**

Membership is based on labor contribution, not capital ownership.

### **Section 8.2 — Membership Units**

Each Member holds one Membership Unit. Units confer governance rights only, carry no economic or equity value, are not transferable or salable, and are extinguished upon a Member's departure.

### **Section 8.3 — Capital Accounts on Departure**

Because Members hold no individual equity interest in the Collective Account, a departing Member is entitled only to:

1. Any earned but undistributed patronage allocation for periods completed prior to departure; and
2. The positive balance, if any, of their individual Capital Account attributable to actual contributions or undistributed allocations under Article V.

No Member is entitled to any share of the Collective Account itself upon departure, whether by resignation, removal, or death.

### **Section 8.4 — Settlement Timing**

Amounts owed under Section 8.3 shall be paid within 90 days of departure, or under an installment plan not exceeding 12 months as approved by ordinary vote, reflecting the Cooperative's cash position. No Departing Member is personally liable for the Cooperative's obligations solely by virtue of former membership.

### **Section 8.5 — Unclaimed Amounts**

If a Departing Member cannot be located to receive an amount owed under Section 8.3, the Cooperative shall hold the amount for 3 years, making reasonable efforts to contact the Member at their last known contact information, after which the unclaimed amount shall be transferred to the Collective Account.

---

## **ARTICLE IX — AMENDMENTS**

These bylaws may be amended by two-thirds vote of Members, subject to Section 4.1(b).

---

## **ARTICLE X — DISSOLUTION**

### **Section 10.1 — Decision to Dissolve**

Dissolution requires a two-thirds Member vote under Section 4.1(b).

### **Section 10.2 — Order of Repayment**

Upon dissolution, assets shall be applied in the following order:

1. Debts and obligations owed to non-member creditors (vendors, tax authorities, lenders, contractors, etc.).
2. Settlement of outstanding Member Capital Account balances under Article VIII.
3. Any remaining assets, including the balance of the Collective Account, distributed as determined by two-thirds Member vote — which may include distribution among Members in proportion to historical patronage, or donation to a cooperative-supporting or Free Software organization.

### **Section 10.3 — No Personal Liability**

Consistent with the Cooperative's status as a limited liability company, no Member shall be personally liable for the Cooperative's remaining debts solely by virtue of membership.

## **ARTICLE XI — CONVERSION TO MULTI-MEMBER LLC & CONTINUING STRUCTURAL EVOLUTION**

### **Section 11.1 — Statement of Completion**

This Article records that, pursuant to the Statement of Intent originally set out in this Article (preserved for historical reference in Section 11.7), the Cooperative converted from a single-member LLC to a multi-member LLC taxed as a partnership, effective **[CONVERSION DATE]**, through: (a) amendment and restatement of the Operating Agreement to admit additional Members, executed consistent with the Wyoming LLC Act, and (b) adoption of these Bylaws and the Member Agreement by the initial admitted Members.

### **Section 11.2 — Non-Dominance of Manager(s) of Record**

The Member(s) serving as statutory Manager(s) of Record under Article VI, Section 6.2 shall act as fiduciary stewards of the Cooperative:

1. They shall not exercise unilateral authority in contradiction of duly adopted Member votes, except where legally required.
2. In the event of conflict between statutory authority and a Cooperative vote, they must disclose the conflict transparently and seek Member direction.

This role is administrative and legal in nature and does not supersede democratic governance.

### **Section 11.3 — International Membership Equity Commitment**

Because a majority of Members may reside outside the United States, the Cooperative affirms:

1. Membership rights shall not depend on U.S. citizenship or residency.
2. Governance rights shall not be conditioned on statutory capital contribution.
3. Where legal or tax constraints affect a non-U.S. Member's participation (see Section 5.6), the Cooperative will work to preserve equivalent governance and economic rights to the fullest extent legally possible.

### **Section 11.4 — Tax & Regulatory Compliance Acknowledgment**

Admitting non-U.S. Members to a partnership-taxed LLC carries specific U.S. tax withholding and reporting obligations, including withholding on effectively connected income allocable to foreign partners under IRC § 1446, and each Member's individual obligations under their own country's tax laws. The Cooperative commits to engaging qualified U.S. tax counsel and accounting professionals to maintain compliance and to minimizing Member-level tax burden to the extent legally possible, and will share relevant findings transparently with Members.

### **Section 11.5 — Continuing Research & Evaluation**

The Cooperative may continue to research further structural evolution beyond its current multi-member LLC form, including but not limited to:

* Conversion to a U.S. worker cooperative corporation
* Election as S-Corporation (if appropriate and consistent with international membership)
* Creation of an international cooperative association
* Multi-entity structure (e.g., U.S. entity + international cooperative)
* Federation model with member-owned local entities
* Steward-ownership or trust-based models

Research shall evaluate legal feasibility across member jurisdictions, tax implications, administrative burden, democratic integrity, protection of Member rights, ease of future Member admission, and risk mitigation. All findings shall be shared transparently with Members.

### **Section 11.6 — Member Decision Process for Further Restructuring**

1. A formal proposal shall be drafted and distributed.
2. Members shall be given adequate time for review and discussion.
3. Adoption of a further restructuring plan requires a two-thirds majority vote under Section 4.1(b).

No further structural conversion shall occur without democratic approval.

### **Section 11.7 — Prior Intent (Historical Record)**

The Cooperative previously affirmed, prior to the conversion recorded in Section 11.1, its intent to transition from a single-member LLC structure into a legally recognized cooperative or cooperative-equivalent structure consistent with democratic member control, member economic participation, transparency of finances, equitable surplus distribution, and non-investor-dominant governance. That prior intent is preserved here as the historical basis for the conversion completed under this Article, and remains the guiding standard for any further evolution under Section 11.5.

### **Section 11.8 — Preservation of Cooperative Rights During Any Future Transition**

Until any further formal conversion under Section 11.5–11.6 is complete:

* One member, one vote remains binding.
* Surplus distribution shall continue under Article V.
* No dilution of governance rights shall occur.
* No equity or capital structure shall be introduced that undermines democratic control.

### **Section 11.9 — Failure to Reach Consensus**

If no legally viable further structure can be identified that preserves democratic control, protects international Member rights, and maintains operational feasibility, the Cooperative may consider dissolution and reformation in another jurisdiction, or creation of a new entity governed by these principles, subject to a two-thirds majority vote.
